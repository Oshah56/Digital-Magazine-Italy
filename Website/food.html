History

Italian cuisine has evolved over centuries, shaped by geographical, economic, and political changes. From the Roman Empire's opulence to the simplicity of peasant meals, Italian food reflects a history where dishes were developed out of necessity and creativity. The use of tomatoes, introduced from the Americas in the 16th century, revolutionized the cuisine, while trade routes introduced spices and new cooking methods. The Renaissance period also played a crucial role in developing fine dining and the art of cooking.
